# ElevenLabs Dracula Sample Pronunciation Notes

Use restrained literary narration. The voice should sound calm, intelligent,
and lightly gothic without melodrama.

## Names And Places

- Jonathan Harker: clear English pronunciation; do not overdramatize.
- Bistritz: pronounce as "BIS-trits" unless owner selects another guide.
- Buda-Pesth: preserve the hyphenated historical form; a slight pause is fine.
- Klausenburgh: pronounce slowly and clearly.
- Hotel Royale: light French ending is acceptable, but clarity matters more.
- Carpathians: "kar-PAY-thee-ans".
- Transylvania: clear four-syllable pronunciation.
- Moldavia: "mol-DAY-vee-uh".
- Bukovina: "boo-koh-VEE-nuh".
- Dracula: restrained, not theatrical.
- Mina: "MEE-nuh".

## Delivery Notes

- Pause briefly after date/location lines.
- Keep diary entries intimate and controlled.
- Preserve punctuation-aware pauses.
- Avoid horror-trailer intensity.
- Avoid comedy, whispering, or shouting.
