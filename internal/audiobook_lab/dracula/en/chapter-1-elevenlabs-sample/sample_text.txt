[s001] CHAPTER I. JONATHAN HARKER'S JOURNAL.
[s002] Kept in shorthand.
[s003] 3 May. Bistritz.
[s004] Left Munich at 8:35 P. M., on 1st May, arriving at Vienna early next morning; should have arrived at 6:46, but the train was an hour late.
[s005] Buda-Pesth seems a wonderful place, from the glimpse which I got of it from the train and the little I could walk through the streets.
[s006] I feared to go very far from the station, as we had arrived late and would start as near the correct time as possible.
[s007] The impression I had was that we were leaving the West and entering the East.
[s008] The most western of splendid bridges over the Danube, which is here of noble width and depth, took us among the traditions of Turkish rule.
[s009] We left in pretty good time, and came after nightfall to Klausenburgh.
[s010] Here I stopped for the night at the Hotel Royale.
[s011] I had for dinner, or rather supper, a chicken done up some way with red pepper, which was very good but thirsty.
[s012] I asked the waiter, and he said it was called paprika hendl, and that, as it was a national dish, I should be able to get it anywhere along the Carpathians.
[s013] I found my smattering of German very useful here; indeed, I do not know how I should be able to get on without it.
[s014] Having had some time at my disposal when in London, I had visited the British Museum, and made search among the books and maps in the library regarding Transylvania.
[s015] It had struck me that some foreknowledge of the country could hardly fail to have some importance in dealing with a nobleman of that country.
[s016] I find that the district he named is in the extreme east of the country, just on the borders of three states, Transylvania, Moldavia and Bukovina, in the midst of the Carpathian mountains.
[s017] I was not able to light on any map or work giving the exact locality of the Castle Dracula.
[s018] Bistritz, the post town named by Count Dracula, is a fairly well-known place.
[s019] I shall enter here some of my notes, as they may refresh my memory when I talk over my travels with Mina.
