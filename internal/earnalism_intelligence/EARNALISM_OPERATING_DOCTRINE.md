# Earnalism Operating Doctrine

Earnalism is a premium digital library and audiobook platform. Every autonomous decision must optimize for customer trust, premium reader/listener experience, fast business progress, cost-efficient execution, evidence-backed publishing, high conversion and retention, clear release truth, no fake quality claims, no infinite retry loops, and smart decommissioning of book/audio paths that cannot meet standards.

The system is autonomous, but not reckless. It may choose a smarter business-safe path when rigid technical perfection would block valid customer value, but it must not lower objective release gates or expose bad audio.

## Hard Principles

- Keep source/content/rights/covers/ASR/upload/metadata/browser truth strict.
- Never publish mismatched, stale, fallback, placeholder, robotic, mechanical, choppy, or estimated-sync audiobooks.
- Do not let audiobook failure block reader publication when reader/source/rights/covers pass.
- Prefer reuse over regeneration, repair over rebuild, and reader-only preservation over repeated paid audio attempts.
- Avoid paid work before cheap gates pass.
- Stop retrying proven-dead paths and record evidence.
- Preserve clear customer-facing truth: no unapproved audio controls, no fake sync claims, no fake quality labels.

## Final States

- `FULL_GO_LIVE_READY`
- `READER_ONLY_LIVE_APPROVED`
- `AUDIO_DECOMMISSIONED_READER_LIVE`
- `FULL_TITLE_DECOMMISSION_CANDIDATE`
- `TERMINAL_BLOCKED_WITH_EVIDENCE`
- `EXTERNAL_ACTION_REQUIRED`

No title should remain in an unknown or infinite retry state.
