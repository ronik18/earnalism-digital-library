# Figma Design Doctrine

Earnalism should feel like a refined bilingual literary room: quiet, editorial, truthful, accessible, fast, culturally native, and commercially clear.

## Product Strategy

Earnalism wins by making readers and listeners feel that every book is cared for. The Home page establishes brand taste and trust immediately. The Library feels curated, not dumped into a grid. Book Detail makes availability truthful and elegant. The Reader feels like a calm reading room. The Audiobook Player appears only when production evidence proves that the audiobook is approved.

Conversion comes from confidence, not pressure. Calls to action should guide users toward reading, listening, saving, subscribing, or exploring through clear hierarchy rather than aggressive visual noise.

## Visual Direction

- Calm luxury literary sanctuary.
- Editorial hierarchy with generous spacing and restrained type.
- Bengali and English books represented with equal dignity.
- Graphical book covers and content-themed motifs, never plain typographic blocks.
- A restrained palette anchored in ivory, paper, espresso, burgundy, gold, sage, and indigo ink.
- Motion only when it improves orientation or perceived quality.

## Anti-Patterns

- Generic SaaS hero sections.
- Dracula-first brand identity.
- Oversized hero typography that makes the page feel claustrophobic.
- White text on pale backgrounds.
- Loud gradients or random accents outside the approved token system.
- Warehoused catalog grids with no editorial grouping.
- Audiobook CTAs that outrun release evidence.
