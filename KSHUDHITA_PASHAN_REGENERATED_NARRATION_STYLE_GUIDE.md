# Kshudhita Pashan Regenerated Narration Style Guide

Status: internal draft. No voice generation occurs in this PR.

## Target Tone

- Literary Bengali
- Intimate
- Restrained
- Eerie
- Premium
- Human-like but truthful
- Not theatrical
- Not cartoonish
- Not robotic
- Not melodramatic

## Punctuation Prosody

- Comma: short breath pause
- Full stop / দাঁড়ি: medium closure pause
- Paragraph break: longer reflective pause
- Ellipsis: suspense or hesitation
- Question mark: controlled rising curiosity
- Exclamation: contained emotional intensity
- Quotation: gentle speaker separation
- Dash: interruption or thought shift
- Scene transition: longer silence, but no dead air

## Emotion Rules

- Happiness: subtle warmth
- Sorrow: slowed cadence, breath weight
- Fear: quiet tension, shorter phrases, controlled pauses
- Anger: sharper rhythm, not shouting
- Crying or sobbing: only if text explicitly supports it; subtle, not artificial
- Laughing: only if text explicitly supports it; restrained
- Suspense: silence and lowered energy, not horror-movie exaggeration

## Pronunciation Expectations

- Preserve Bengali vowels and conjunct consonants clearly.
- Avoid casual flattening of Tagore-era literary phrasing.
- Flag Sanskritized or archaic words for human listening QA.
- Keep English or Persian-origin terms natural, not over-Anglicized.

## Bengali Diction Expectations

- Use refined literary diction suitable for Rabindranath Tagore.
- Avoid overly modern colloquial delivery.
- Avoid caricatured gothic performance.

## Tagore-Era Literary Sensitivity

- Treat the text as literary fiction, not a horror trailer.
- Preserve restraint, ambiguity, and atmosphere.
- Let silence and cadence carry mood before adding emotional force.

## Chapter Consistency

- Maintain the same narrator identity across chapters.
- Keep pacing, pitch range, and pause behavior consistent.
- Human QA must compare chapter openings and endings for continuity.

## Mobile And Headphone Listening

- Voice must remain intelligible on small phone speakers.
- Headphone playback must not exaggerate breath, sibilance, or mouth noise.
- Long silences must feel intentional and must not appear as playback failure.
